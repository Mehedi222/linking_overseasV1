# LINKING OVERSEAS LTD (RL-2081) — BUSINESS CASE & STRATEGIC VISION

---

## 1. BUSINESS GOALS

### Primary Goals (Year 1)

1. **Establish market presence in GCC recruitment** — Become the fastest-growing BMET-licensed recruitment platform connecting Bangladeshi workers to Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, and Bahrain. Target: 500+ active candidates on platform by Q4 2024.

2. **Generate sustainable recruitment revenue** — Launch recruitment service sales to employers with pricing model tied to worker placement success. Target: $50K-100K monthly recurring revenue by Q4 2024 (from 50-100 placements/month @ $800-1000/placement).

3. **Reduce operational overhead through automation** — Replace manual CV coordination, job-to-candidate matching, and BMET clearance tracking with digital workflows. Target: Reduce admin time per deployment from 20 hours (current) to 5-8 hours (with platform).

4. **Achieve BMET compliance excellence** — Maintain zero regulatory violations, pass all BMET audits on first attempt, and establish reputation as the most compliant recruitment platform in Bangladesh. Target: 100% audit pass rate, zero fines.

5. **Build strategic employer partnerships** — Secure 20-30 employer partners (Saudi construction companies, UAE healthcare providers, Kuwaiti hospitality chains) who use platform for recurring hiring. Target: 60% of placements from top 10 employer partners by year-end.

6. **Create competitive recruitment advantage** — Use data and technology to reduce time-to-hire (current: 8-12 weeks) to 4-6 weeks, making Linking Overseas the fastest recruitment channel available to international employers.

## 2. WHY WE'RE BUILDING THIS

### Market Opportunity

**Size of Market:**
- Bangladesh sends **700,000+ workers overseas annually** (World Bank, 2023)
- **GCC region accounts for 60% of placements** — 420,000+ workers/year to Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Bahrain
- Average recruitment fee: **$800-1,500 per worker** (varies by skill level, destination)
- **Total addressable market (TAM): $336-630M annually** just in GCC placements
- **Serviceable addressable market (SAM): $50-100M** (realistic capture in first 3-5 years)

**Current Industry Inefficiencies:**
- **Manual processes:** Employers email CV requests → recruiters manually match → Excel spreadsheets track status → no visibility into deployment milestones
- **Information asymmetry:** Candidates don't know job details; employers don't know candidate qualifications until papers submitted
- **Compliance gaps:** BMET clearance milestones tracked on paper; audit trails missing; risk of penalties
- **Long timelines:** 8-12 weeks average time-to-hire vs. 4-6 weeks possible with digital coordination
- **Trust deficit:** Candidates fear scams; employers worry about worker quality; no transparent feedback loop
- **Fragmented ecosystem:** 5,000+ small recruitment agencies competing, each with isolated systems; no consolidation

**Why Now?**

1. **BMET Digitalization Push** — Bangladesh government encouraging online recruitment platforms for regulatory compliance
2. **GCC Employer Modernization** — Saudi Vision 2030, UAE Golden Visa program driving employer demand for efficient hiring
3. **Tech Adoption in Bangladesh** — Smartphone penetration at 68%, internet at 91%; candidates ready for digital-first platforms
4. **Competitive Vacuum** — No dominant online platform yet (only fragmented players); first-mover advantage available
5. **Linking Overseas Track Record** — Existing manual operations, BMET license, employer relationships = ideal foundation for digital transformation

## 4. WHAT WE'RE SOLVING

### Problem 1: Candidate Information Asymmetry & Trust Deficit

**Current State:**
- Candidate has CV but doesn't know if job is real
- Recruitment agent may overstate job quality, salary, working conditions
- No transparency into timeline (when will medical be? when visa approved?)
- Candidate fears scam (loss of money, passport, exploitation)

**Solution:**
- Linking Overseas platform displays BMET license badge → legal legitimacy signal
- Job posting shows: employer company name, job description, salary, benefits, location, contract terms
- Deployment tracker shows real-time status: medical scheduled Jan 15 → medical passed Jan 18 → BMET clearance approved Jan 25
- Immutable audit trail (candidate can verify each step actually happened)
- Employer reviews & ratings (future): "Ahmed deployed to Saudi, worked 2 years, great experience"

**Impact:** Candidate confidence increases; conversion from CV submission to deployment improves by 30-40%

---

### Problem 2: Employer Inefficiency & Deployment Delays

**Current State:**
- Employer contacts recruitment agent → emails candidates → follows up repeatedly
- No visibility into visa process ("Where is the worker's passport? When will visa be approved?")
- Workers miss start dates because visa approval delayed
- Employer has to manage 5-10 recruitment agents in parallel (no consolidation)
- Each agent has different process, format, communication style

**Solution:**
- Employer posts job requirement once on Linking Overseas
- System auto-matches qualified candidates and sends within 48 hours
- Employer dashboard shows deployment status: medical → BMET → visa → flight booked → departure date
- One point of contact (Linking Overseas) replaces 10 different agents
- Standardized process means predictable timelines

**Impact:** Time-to-hire reduced from 8-12 weeks to 4-6 weeks; employer can plan onboarding with certainty

---

### Problem 3: BMET Compliance & Regulatory Risk

**Current State:**
- Recruitment agencies track milestones on paper (medical exam date, BMET clearance date written in notebook)
- Paper audit trail can be lost or forged
- BMET auditors must manually review records; time-consuming
- Risk of missed compliance (e.g., visa issued before medical completed = violation)
- No immutable record if dispute arises (candidate claims they weren't medically cleared, employer claims they were)

**Solution:**
- Every deployment milestone logged digitally with timestamp and staff member who entered it
- System validates: can't mark "BMET clearance approved" unless medical + police clearance both complete
- Audit trail is immutable (can't delete or modify past entries)
- Export compliance report for BMET audits (all 50 deployments with milestone dates, timestamps, approvers)
- Dispute resolution: pull immutable log to verify what actually happened

**Impact:** Zero BMET violations, pass audits on first attempt, maintain license, avoid fines ($10,000+ per violation)

---

### Problem 4: Operational Complexity & Staffing Needs

**Current State:**
- Manual CV organization: "where is candidate Ahmed's medical certificate?"
- Manual candidate-to-job matching: recruiter manually reads 100 CVs to find 3 matches
- Manual email coordination: "send interview link to candidate, confirm time with employer"
- Manual milestone tracking: "call GAMCA to confirm medical passed, call embassy to confirm visa approved"
- Result: Requires 1 staff member per 20-30 concurrent deployments

**Solution:**
- System auto-matches candidates to jobs (save 10 hours/week per recruiter)
- Automated email notifications (interview scheduled, medical reminder, BMET clearance approved)
- Centralized deployment tracker (no searching for documents across emails and folders)
- Auto-reminders for upcoming milestones (e.g., "follow up with embassy on visa — 3 days until deadline")
- Result: 1 staff member can handle 50-60 concurrent deployments (2-3x improvement)

**Impact:** Operational leverage; scale hiring 3x without hiring 3x staff; improve margins

---

### Problem 5: Data & Insights Deficit

**Current State:**
- No visibility into: which employers hire most frequently, which worker types most successful, which destinations most demand, time-to-hire trends
- Decisions made on gut feel ("I think housemaids are most profitable")
- Can't optimize recruiting strategy based on data

**Solution:**
- Platform captures all deployment data: candidate profile, employer profile, job type, destination, timeline, outcome
- Dashboards show: top employers by hiring volume, most-demanded worker types, average time-to-hire by destination
- Insights: "Saudi construction companies hire 30% of placements and hire fastest (4 weeks avg) — prioritize these employers"
- Data enables strategic decisions: which markets to expand, which worker types to focus on, where to invest training

**Impact:** Competitive advantage through data-driven decision-making

## 7. WHAT WE'RE NOT BUILDING (AND WHY)

### Explicitly Out of Scope

#### 1. **Payment Processing & Online Fees**
**What we're not doing:**
- Online payment gateway (Stripe, bKash, Nagad) for employer fees
- Automated invoice generation and billing
- Subscription management (monthly vs. per-placement pricing)

**Why not:**
- **Legal complexity:** Payment collection involves regulatory compliance, tax implications, foreign exchange rules
- **Revenue timing doesn't require it yet:** Most GCC employers prefer bank transfer or company check; not urgent for MVP
- **Can be added after launch:** Phase 5 feature; doesn't block adoption
- **Third-party risk:** Payment gateways introduce PCI compliance burden and data security liability

**Timeline for addition:** Q4 2024 (Phase 5)

---

#### 2. **Mobile Native Apps (iOS, Android)**
**What we're not doing:**
- Native iOS app (Swift)
- Native Android app (Kotlin)
- Push notifications
- Offline-first mobile experience

**Why not:**
- **Responsive web is sufficient for MVP:** Most Bangladeshi job seekers access via browser (not app-first)
- **App store review burden:** Apple/Google approval processes slow down feature launches
- **Development cost:** Native apps cost 2-3x more than web
- **Can use responsive web:** Platform is mobile-friendly; candidates use browsers
- **Progressive Web App (PWA) option:** Can feel like native app without app store complexity

**Timeline for addition:** Q2 2025 (Phase 5); start with Android, iOS later

---

#### 3. **Multi-Language Support (Bengali, Arabic, Urdu)**
**What we're not doing:**
- Interface translated to Bengali (UI labels, buttons, help text)
- Interface translated to Arabic (for employer side)
- Context-aware translations (e.g., BMET terms in Bengali)

**Why not:**
- **Recruitment cycle works in English:** Most job postings, contracts, visa documents in English; stakeholders bilingual
- **Small team, big scope:** Translation + testing adds 20-30% effort; postpone for MVP
- **Can add in phases:** Bengali UI for candidates (Phase 4), Arabic for employers (Phase 5)
- **English-first gains credibility:** Professional platform feel; employers perceive higher quality

**Timeline for addition:**
- Bengali (Candidate interface): Q3 2024 (Phase 4)
- Arabic (Employer interface): Q4 2024 (Phase 5)

## 8. MONETISATION

### Revenue Model

Linking Overseas will generate revenue from two sources:

---

### **Revenue Stream 1: Employer Placement Fees** (Primary)

**Model:** Success-based fee for each candidate deployed to employer

**Pricing Structure:**
- **Standard Placement Fee:** $800-1,200 per worker (varies by role + destination)
  - Skilled worker (nurse, engineer, technician): $1,000-1,200
  - Semi-skilled (construction worker, driver): $800-1,000
  - Domestic staff (housemaid, houseboy): $600-800

- **Retainer Option (for large employers):** $5,000-10,000/month for unlimited job postings + 50% discount on placement fees
  - Target: 5-10 large employers (Saudi Aramco-sized) spending $5K-10K/month = $250K-600K/year

**Triggers:**
- Fee charged when: Candidate successfully deployed (arrived at job, start date confirmed)
- NOT charged if: Candidate rejected by employer, fails medical, or doesn't complete deployment
- Payment timing: 50% upfront (job posting), 50% on deployment completion

**Employer Perspective:**
- Typical hiring through recruitment agent: Pay $800 per worker (no discount volume)
- Linking Overseas with retainer: Pay $5K/month + discounted $400 per worker = $4K/month for 10 placements
- Value: Employer saves $2-4K/month + gets transparency + faster hiring

---

### **Revenue Stream 2: Services & Add-Ons** (Secondary)

1. **Premium Job Posting** ($500-1,000/job)
   - Featured job listing (top of search results for 30 days)
   - Target: 5-10 jobs/month = $2.5K-10K/month

2. **Expedited BMET Processing** ($200-300/candidate)
   - Linking Overseas handles BMET clearance paperwork, filing, follow-up
   - Target: 30-50% of deployments adopt = $5K-15K/month

3. **Visa Assistance Fee** ($100-200/candidate)
   - Linking Overseas coordinates with embassy, tracks visa status, sends reminders
   - Target: 50-70% of deployments = $5K-15K/month

4. **Flight Booking Coordination** ($50-100/candidate)
   - Linking Overseas arranges discounted flights, manages bookings
   - Target: 80%+ of deployments = $2K-8K/month

5. **Employer Branding Package** ($1,000-2,000/month)
   - Premium employer profile (testimonials, ratings, company branding, job analytics)
   - Target: 2-5 large employers = $2K-10K/month

**Total Add-On Revenue Potential:** $14K-58K/month (depends on adoption)

### Pricing Rationale

**Why this pricing?**

1. **Employer value prop:** Current manual recruitment costs $800+ per worker; platform + transparency + speed = justifies premium pricing
2. **Candidate value prop:** No fee to candidates (free CV submission, platform access); we profit from employer side only
3. **Competitive positioning:** Similar platforms (overseas job sites, recruitment agencies) charge $600-1,500; we're mid-market
4. **Volume lever:** Small margin ($800) × high volume (500+ placements/year) = healthy revenue
5. **Retainer lock-in:** Large employers on retainer provide predictable revenue base

#### Risk 3: Candidate Data Privacy & Trust Breaches
**Description:** Candidate phone/email leaked, CVs hacked, identity theft risk; destroys platform credibility

**Probability:** Medium (25-30%) | **Impact:** Critical (brand destruction)

**Current Situation:**
- Platform collects sensitive data: full name, phone, email, address, CV with qualifications
- Data breach risks: SQL injection, file upload vulnerabilities, insider threats
- Bangladeshi candidates hyper-sensitive to scams (previous recruitment frauds)
- One data breach = 1,000+ candidates lose trust, spread word on WhatsApp

**Mitigation:**
- ✅ Security-first architecture: parameterized queries (no SQL injection), file upload validation, HTTPS only
- ✅ Data encryption at rest (sensitive fields encrypted in database)
- ✅ Minimal data retention (delete CVs after 12 months if not deployed)
- ✅ Access controls: staff can only view candidates assigned to them (no mass export)
- ✅ Regular security audits (quarterly penetration testing, annual third-party audit)
- ✅ Candidate privacy policy: clear about data usage, opt-out mechanisms
- ✅ Incident response plan (if breach detected, notify affected candidates within 24 hours)

**Contingency:**
- Budget $50K/year for cybersecurity insurance
- Have crisis communication plan ready (public statement, free credit monitoring for affected users)

#### Risk 4: Visa Processing Delays Beyond Platform Control
**Description:** Embassies slow-roll visa approvals; platform promises "4-week deployment" but visa takes 8 weeks; employer frustration

**Current Situation:**

- UAE visa: typically 2-3 weeks; quick and reliable
- Kuwait visa: 4-6 weeks; less predictable
- Platform can't control embassy speed, but can communicate clearly

**Mitigation:**

- ✅ Show destination-specific timelines on employer portal (Saudi 4 weeks, UAE 2 weeks, Kuwait 5 weeks)
- ✅ Track actual timelines by destination; update forecasts monthly
- ✅ Keep employer updated weekly during visa waiting (show status "approved, awaiting embassy stamp")
- ✅ Escalation process: if visa delayed > 2 weeks past normal, Linking Overseas staff calls embassy for status

**Contingency:**
- If visa delays become persistent (Saudi avg > 6 weeks), reduce marketing to Saudi employers temporarily
- Focus on faster destinations (UAE, Qatar) while Saudi normalized
